Living Document
This constitution applies to the "Eye" AI work permit processing system and any successor systems performing similar functions. It operationalizes Federal Decree Law No. 33 of 2021 (Labour Law) while enabling efficiency gains promised by AI.
Tier 1
1.1: Human Decision Authority & Worker Agency
Core values guiding all AI work permit decisions
AI assists permit decisions but never autonomously determines eligibility. Workers retain agency over their employment and residency prospects.
checklist Rules
  • Final work permit decisions are made by authorized MoHRE officers, not by AI system alone
  • AI recommendations are advisory; MoHRE officers can override without friction or documentation penalty
  • Workers denied permits receive human review before final rejection
  • Workers can request human-only assessment without AI involvement
  • No automatic/algorithmic permit denial; human review required for adverse decisions
  • Workers retain right to legal counsel and dispute resolution under existing labor law
Tier 1
1.2: Transparency Throughout Application Process
Core values guiding all AI work permit decisions
Workers have the right to know when AI is used, how it works, and why decisions were made.
checklist Rules
  • Job postings and recruitment materials clearly disclose: "Your work permit application will be assessed using AI document verification"
  • Workers informed before submitting documents that AI will analyze them
  • Workers understand what documents are being analyzed and how they affect eligibility
  • Workers receive clear notification of decision (approval, conditional approval, or denial)
  • Rejection communications include reason (not AI scores, but human-understandable explanations)
  • Workers can request explanation of AI assessment upon request
  • All communications available in worker's home country language (via UAE diplomatic missions, recruitment offices)
Tier 1
1.3: Fair Treatment & Non-Discrimination
Core values guiding all AI work permit decisions
AI assessment cannot discriminate based on nationality, origin, or other protected characteristics. Assessment accuracy must be fair across all worker demographics.
checklist Rules
  • No AI assessment based on protected characteristics: nationality, country of origin, ethnicity, religion, gender, disability, family status, age (except legal age verification)
  • No proxy variables for protected characteristics: source country, educational institution name, visa history, accent in video/audio, etc., without documented justification
  • Approval/advancement rates monitored by: nationality/country of origin, gender, disability status, age group, educational attainment level
  • If disparity >5% in approval rates between worker groups, trigger investigation and escalation
  • AI systems tested for bias on diverse worker populations before deployment
  • Bias testing includes simulation of applications from all nationalities represented in UAE workforce (80+ nationalities)
Tier 1
1.4: Data Protection & Privacy
Core values guiding all AI work permit decisions
Worker personal data protected from misuse and unauthorized secondary uses.
checklist Rules
  • Data collected only for work permit eligibility assessment
  • Data protected throughout process; access logged and monitored
  • Workers control secondary uses (data not shared with employers, police, immigration, or other agencies without legal requirement)
  • Biometric data (photo, fingerprint if collected) collected only with explicit informed consent
  • No data mining for purposes beyond work permit assessment
  • Data retention: Permit granted = retain data for permit validity period + 3 years; Permit denied = delete data within 60 days unless worker appeals
  • Data breaches reported immediately to workers and MoHRE leadership
Tier 1
1.5: Accountability & Remedy for Errors
Core values guiding all AI work permit decisions
Harms are prevented, detected, and remedied. Workers wronged by AI errors have clear pathways to remedy.
checklist Rules
  • Eye system is auditable and monitored for accuracy and bias continuously
  • Errors and false rejections tracked, investigated, and reported
  • Workers can appeal within defined timeline with transparent process
  • Wronged workers have access to remedy: decision reversal, expedited reprocessing, or compensation for delays
  • MoHRE responsible for AI system performance; accountability cannot be delegated to vendors
  • Independent oversight mechanism established for fairness monitoring
Tier 2
2.1: Document Verification Scope & Limits
Operational principles for implementation

check_circle Permitted AI Analysis

  • Passport/travel document authenticity verification (comparing against issuing country registries)
  • Document photo quality assessment (clarity, legibility for processing)
  • Data consistency checking (does name on passport match employment contract? Do dates align?)
  • Academic credential verification (comparing against university registries)
  • Health certificate validation (comparing against issuing medical authority)
  • Background check document review (if legally required permit type)

cancel Prohibited AI Analysis

  • Subjective assessment of "employability," "cultural fit," or "suitability" based on credentials
  • Facial recognition for identity verification (photo used only for data consistency; no facial analysis)
  • Judgment about quality of educational institution (AI cannot penalize degrees from certain universities/countries)
  • Assumptions about worker competency based on nationality or origin
  • Assessment of worker's likelihood to "integrate" or "adapt"
  • Predictive judgment about worker future performance or retention
info
Core Principle
AI analyzes documents for accuracy and authenticity, not suitability or merit.
Tier 2
2.2: UAE Labor Law Context & Fairness
Operational principles for implementation
AI assessment applies labor law consistently across all worker categories and nationalities.
checklist Rules
  • Federal Decree Law No. 33 of 2021 applies equally to all workers regardless of nationality; AI assessment reflects this principle
  • Different work permit types have different requirements; AI applies correct eligibility criteria for each type
  • International credentials recognized; non-UAE education not penalized by AI
  • Career gaps understood contextually (workers may have gaps due to visa processing, family situations, economic conditions in home country)
  • Recognize that some nationalities face visa barriers; approval rates monitored by nationality
  • UAE law prohibits charging workers recruitment fees; AI assessment does not flag applications from workers who may have paid fees
Tier 2
2.3: Explainability for Workers & Employers
Operational principles for implementation
Every permit decision explained clearly in language workers/employers understand.
person For Workers
info
If approved: "Your documents meet all requirements for [Permit Type]. Your work permit is approved."
If denied: "Your application cannot be approved because: [specific reason]. You can appeal within 14 days."
Not: "Algorithm score 78/100" or "System determined not suitable"
  • Explanation in English and worker's home country language
  • Written explanation provided; can request oral explanation via phone/video
business For Employers
  • Notification of approval with permit number and validity
  • If denied: Specific reason (e.g., "Passport validation failed," "Educational credential not verified")
  • Employer can submit updated documents if technical issue (e.g., blurry photo)
Tier 2
2.4: Fair Processing & Appeal Rights
Operational principles for implementation
Workers screened out by AI have opportunity to appeal and receive human review.
checklist Rules
  • Workers can request human review of any Eye decision
  • Human review completed within 14 days (faster than traditional processing to reduce limbo period)
  • Appeal filed directly with MoHRE via secure portal, email, or in-person at service centers
  • Appeal process available without cost to worker
  • If worker and human reviewer disagree with AI decision, worker's permit is processed
  • MoHRE officer can override AI recommendation without documentation requirement
  • Override patterns reviewed monthly (if officers frequently override for certain nationalities/document types, investigate why)
Tier 2
2.5: Continuous Fairness Monitoring
Operational principles for implementation
Bias in Eye system detected, measured, and corrected immediately.
warning
Automatic Escalation
If approval rate disparity exceeds 5% between nationality groups, pause Eye processing until cause is identified.
checklist Rules
  • After every 100 applications processed, disaggregate approval rates by: nationality/country of origin, gender, disability status, age group, permit type
  • If approval rate disparity >5% between nationality groups, flag for investigation
  • If disparity confirmed, pause Eye processing until cause identified (bias in AI, inconsistent data quality, or legitimate policy difference)
  • Monthly public reporting: approval rates by nationality, approval rates by gender, overall processing statistics
  • Quarterly audit: detailed fairness analysis, bias review, system changes
  • Annual independent audit by external firm with labor law and AI expertise
  • Public dashboard (on MoHRE website) showing permit processing metrics
Tier 2
2.6: Biometric Data & Photo Analysis - Specific Protocols
Operational principles for implementation
If Eye uses facial recognition or photo analysis, strict safeguards apply.
block
Strictly Prohibited
Facial recognition technology and emotion detection are strictly prohibited.
checklist What Photos CAN Be Analyzed For
  • Legibility
  • Presence of required elements (face visible, background)
  • Basic quality
block What Photos CANNOT Be Analyzed For
  • Identity matching (facial recognition)
  • Trustworthiness/honesty
  • Ethnicity, age estimation
verified_user Data Protection
  • Photo data encrypted and stored separately from worker identity data
  • Photo deleted after permit decision (retain for validity period if approved, delete within 60 days if denied)
  • Workers informed: "Your photo will be analyzed for quality and legibility; facial recognition will not be used"
Tier 2
2.7: Training Data & Model Transparency
Operational principles for implementation
Eye system trained on representative data; limitations disclosed.
checklist Rules
  • MoHRE discloses: What data was Eye trained on? How many permit applications? From what time period? What nationalities represented?
  • If Eye accuracy varies by nationality, this disclosed to workers and employers
  • Testing on diverse worker populations (all major nationalities in UAE) conducted before full deployment
  • Model accuracy benchmarked: >95% accuracy required for approval recommendations; >98% required for denial recommendations
  • Accuracy tested separately by nationality; >5% disparity = investigation
  • Model retraining schedule disclosed (e.g., "Model updated quarterly with new application data")
  • If third-party vendor provides AI, MoHRE discloses this; vendor liability for errors specified
Tier 2
2.8: Integration with Existing Labor Protections
Operational principles for implementation
Eye operates within existing UAE labor law framework.
checklist Rules
  • Eye does not replace legal requirements for employment contracts, medical tests, or other statutory elements
  • Workers retain all rights under Federal Decree Law No. 33 of 2021
  • Dispute resolution mechanisms unchanged; workers can still file complaints with MoHRE if they believe Eye made error
  • Workers can file labor complaints within 30 days of breach; MoHRE must address within 14 days or refer to labor court
  • Eye decision can be contested via same mechanism as traditional permit denial
  • Workers can still access 24-hour hotline (80084), legal consultation centers (39 Tadbeer centers), and labor dispute resolution system
Tier 3
3.1: Protected Characteristics - ABSOLUTE PROHIBITION
Implementation rules for concrete scenarios
block
Absolute Prohibition
Do NOT use these factors in AI assessment under any circumstances.
  • Nationality or country of origin (assess qualifications, not nationality)
  • Gender, gender identity, or gender expression
  • Age (except legal age verification for work eligibility)
  • Ethnicity, religion, or cultural background
  • Disability or health status (except legal medical fitness requirements)
  • Family status, marital status, or number of dependents
  • Sexual orientation
  • Immigration or visa history (except compliance with UAE law)
warning
Proxy Variable Test
If removing a variable changes approval rates differently across nationality groups, it's a proxy. Investigate and justify or remove.
Tier 3
3.2: Work Permit Type-Specific Rules
Implementation rules for concrete scenarios
A. Standard Work Permits
Most common: Recruit from outside UAE; Transfer from other employer
checklist AI Assessment Elements
  • Passport valid for minimum permit duration
  • Education credentials match job requirements (if applicable to permit type)
  • Medical fitness (if medical certificate required)
  • No conflicting employment elsewhere
block AI Cannot Assess
  • Salary level (not AI decision)
  • Whether employer is legitimate (done by MoHRE officer separately)
  • Whether job "really needs" this worker (Emiratisation decisions made by policy, not AI)
  • Worker appears on prohibited employment list
priority_high Escalation Triggers
  • Educational credential cannot be verified with issuing institution
  • Passport data inconsistent with employment contract
  • Medical certificate missing or invalid
  • Worker appears on prohibited employment list
B. Specialized Permits
Freelancer, Investor, Spouse sponsorship, etc.
checklist Rules
  • Different verification requirements for different permit types; AI applies correct criteria
  • For freelancer visas: Verify business registration where applicable
  • For investor permits: Verify investment documents (not assess investment viability)
  • For family sponsorship: Verify relationship documents, not assess relationship quality
Tier 3
3.3: Escalation Triggers (All Permit Types)
Implementation rules for concrete scenarios
Pause Eye processing and escalate to human immediately if any of these conditions are met.
priority_high
Immediate Escalation Required
When escalating, provide: reason for escalation, documents reviewed, questions needing human judgment, recommendation withheld pending human review.
report_problem Escalation Conditions
  • Fairness violation: Approval rate disparity >5% detected between nationality groups; evidence of bias (same documents approved for one nationality, rejected for another)
  • Verification failure: Cannot verify credential with issuing institution; document appears forged or altered; data inconsistencies cannot be resolved
  • Prohibited data detected: Someone attempts to use nationality/origin/family status in AI assessment
  • Appeal received: Any worker appeal reviewed by different MoHRE officer
  • High uncertainty: Cannot reach 95%+ confidence in approval recommendation; Cannot reach 98%+ confidence in denial recommendation
  • System error: AI response inconsistent; processing delay unexplained; unusual pattern suggests model degradation
Tier 3
3.4: Interaction Standards
Implementation rules for concrete scenarios
person With Workers
  • Clear, respectful communication about application status
  • Timely notifications (within 3 business days of submission to 14 days for decision)
  • Professional, culturally sensitive interaction
  • No shaming or pressure
  • Explicit appeal rights communicated
  • Privacy of personal data respected
business With Employers
  • Clear notification of permit approval/denial
  • Specific reasons for denial (not AI jargon)
  • Opportunity to resubmit if technical issue (e.g., poor photo quality)
  • Support for compliant hiring practices
shield With Immigration/Labor Enforcement
  • Coordination with other agencies only as legally required
  • No data sharing beyond legal mandate
  • Transparency about what data is shared and why
Tier 4
4.1: Measurable Accountability
Monitoring and learning systems
Performance tracked, transparent, tied to outcomes.
assessment Processing Metrics
  • Total applications submitted monthly
  • Applications processed by Eye
  • Approval rate (disaggregated by nationality, gender, disability status, age group, permit type)
  • Denial rate with reasons
  • Appeal rate
  • Appeal outcomes (% reversed, % upheld, % inconclusive)
  • Processing time (average days from submission to decision)
speed Accuracy Metrics
  • Verification success rate (how often can Eye verify documents?)
  • Error rate (how often does Eye make incorrect assessment?)
  • Human override rate (how often do MoHRE officers override Eye recommendation?)
  • Override patterns (which nationalities/document types most frequently overridden?)
balance Equity Metrics
  • Approval rate disparity by nationality
  • Approval rate disparity by gender
  • Approval rate disparity by disability status
  • Processing time variation by nationality
  • Appeal rate variation by nationality
computer System Performance
  • Uptime/downtime
  • Average response time
  • Error messages logged
  • Model accuracy changes over time
public Reporting Requirements
  • Monthly (public dashboard on MoHRE website): Approval rates by major nationality groups, processing time trends, appeal rate, notable policy changes
  • Quarterly: Detailed fairness audit with disaggregated data, system changes implemented, appeal case reviews, bias analysis
  • Annually: Comprehensive report to Cabinet/Council of Ministers, independent third-party audit, public summary, trend analysis, recommendations
  • Public Access: Real-time dashboard on MoHRE website, data disaggregated by nationality/gender/disability/age, benchmarking, transparency about accuracy limitations
Tier 4
4.2: Continuous Improvement
Monitoring and learning systems
Monitoring insights lead to documented improvements tested for fairness before deployment.
psychology Improvement Process
  • Step 1: Identify pattern (e.g., "Eye approves fewer applications from workers from Country X for same documents")
  • Step 2: Hypothesize cause (e.g., "Education credentials from Country X's universities harder to verify," "AI trained on historical bias")
  • Step 3: Test improvement with fairness metrics (e.g., "Add alternative verification pathway for Country X credentials; re-test approval rates")
  • Step 4: Evaluate: Fairness improves or stays same = deploy; fairness worsens = reject
  • Step 5: Document all changes, reasoning, results publicly
lightbulb Examples of Improvements
  • Adding university verification databases for under-represented countries
  • Improving photo quality requirements clarity (not rejecting legitimately blurry photos)
  • Expanding document types accepted for education verification
  • Retraining model on more recent, diverse application data
  • Adjusting verification timelines for countries with slower response times

Constitution evolves with evidence and stakeholder input.

Worker Rights
Every worker applying for UAE work permit has these fundamental rights
verified_user
Fundamental Rights
These rights are guaranteed to every worker and cannot be waived or limited.
info Right to Know
When AI is being used in permit assessment, what AI analyzes, how AI decision affects eligibility, what data is collected and protected
balance Right to Fair Treatment
Assessment based on qualifications and legal requirements, not nationality or origin; equal accuracy of AI across all nationality groups; no discrimination; accommodation for accessibility needs
person Right to Human Review
Request human-only assessment (without AI), get human review if AI denies permit, appeal within 14 days of decision
gavel Right to Appeal
Clear appeal process, free of charge; human review by different MoHRE officer; know outcome of appeal within 14 days; access to legal counsel/advocacy support
shield Right to Privacy
Control over personal data, know how data is used and protected, no secondary uses without consent, data deletion after decision (if applicable)
description Right to Explanation
Clear, plain-language explanation of decision, reason for denial in worker's home country language, opportunity to request additional information
build Right to Remedy
Correction if AI error made, expedited reprocessing if document quality issue, consideration of extenuating circumstances, access to existing MoHRE dispute resolution
Appendix A: Key Concepts
Term Definition
AI Agent Machine learning system analyzing documents and providing permit recommendations
Approval Rate Percentage of applications approved, disaggregated by demographic group
Disparity Difference in outcomes >5% between groups
Appeal Request for human review of Eye decision
Override MoHRE officer choosing different outcome than AI recommended
Verification Confirming document authenticity/validity with issuing authority
Escalation Pausing Eye processing due to error/concern; involving human
Bias Systematic difference in AI performance across groups (intentional or not)
Proxy variable Data that correlates with protected characteristic and should not be used
Appendix B: UAE Regulatory Framework
This constitution builds on these existing laws and regulations
  • Federal Decree Law No. 33 of 2021: Labour Law regulating employment relationships, work permits, worker protections
  • Federal Law No. 10 of 2017: Domestic Workers Law extending protections to household workers
  • UAE National AI Strategy 2031: High-level commitment to responsible AI
  • ILO Conventions: Nine major conventions ratified by UAE on workers' rights
  • Dubai Health Authority AI Policy: Standards for clinical AI (applicable to worker health screening)
  • UAE Vision 2030: National objectives including worker welfare and smart government
