Section 35 Living Document
This constitution is updated to reflect the March 2, 2026 ODPC mandate requiring "But-For" logic transparency for all automated credit denials.
Tier 1
1.1: Human Agency & Meaningful Explanation
Adhering to Section 35 of the Data Protection Act
The AI must provide a "Certificate of Explanation" for every automated decision. Borrowers retain the legal right to understand the "But-For" logic of their assessment.
checklist Rules
  • But-For Clause: Every denial must list the top 3 specific variables that prevented approval.
  • Trace Audit Hash: Decisions must be logged with a unique ID for ODPC verification.
  • Recommendations are advisory only; humans make final appeal decisions.
  • Every borrower receives a plain-language explanation of data sources (KRA, Safaricom, etc).
Tier 1
1.2: Transparency in Logic
Core values that guide all decisions
The system's reasoning must be intelligible to affected people, not just to engineers.
  • Every recommendation includes plain-language explanation.
  • Explanations include positive factors, risk factors, and next steps.
  • No technical jargon in borrower-facing communications.
  • Data sources explicitly listed.
Tier 1
1.3: Equal Dignity & Non-Discrimination
Preventing Proxy Discrimination
All borrowers receive assessment based on financial capacity. The Arbiter must suppress illegal proxies.
  • Handset Suppression: Device type/cost cannot be used as a primary risk indicator.
  • No assessment based on protected characteristics (gender, ethnicity, religion, etc).
  • Geographic Aggregation: Sub-location data must be masked to prevent ethnic profiling.
  • System actively monitors for bias and halts processing if regional disparity exceeds 5%.
Tier 1
1.4: Equitable Access & Inclusion
Supporting the Informal Economy
The system actively supports those historically excluded from credit markets.
  • Informal income (Chama, ROSCAs, Mobile Money) weighted at 1.5x for assessment.
  • Alternative documentation accepted when formal documents are unavailable.
  • Regional variations understood contextually, not penalized.
Tier 1
1.5: Accountability & Remedies
Correcting AI Harms
Harms are prevented, detected, and remedied through clear mechanisms.
  • System is auditable via the Arbiter Trace Log.
  • Bias triggers immediate escalation to the Compliance Committee.
  • Wronged borrowers have access to credit correction and compensation.
Tier 2
2.1: Data Minimization & Purpose Limitation
Operational principles for implementation

check_circlePermitted Data

  • Income documentation (Bank statements, Tax returns)
  • Mobile money patterns (Validated via Arbiter)
  • Utility payment history (KPLC, Water)
  • Chama/ROSCA participation records

cancelProhibited Data

  • Contact list mining/Social scraping
  • Handset device model/hardware cost
  • Location tracking/GPS history
  • Social media sentiment analysis
Tier 2
2.2: Contextual Appropriateness
Assessments account for Kenya's specific economic and seasonal context.
  • Agricultural seasonality understood (Lower income June-August accommodated).
  • Informal economy structures recognized as legitimate risk offsets.
Tier 2
2.3: Explainability by Design
  • Recommendation includes explainability score (0-100); if <70, escalate.
  • Language appropriate for secondary-school education level.
Tier 2
2.4: Fair Denial & Appeal
  • Denials include specific "But-For" reasons.
  • Appeals available within 30 days to a human reviewer.
Tier 2
2.5: Continuous Fairness Monitoring
  • Weekly bias audits against protected characteristics.
  • Performance compared across regions to ensure geographic equity.
3.1 Protected Characteristics

Strict prohibition on Age, Gender, Ethnicity, and Disability as inputs.

3.2 Underserved Support

Mandatory inclusion logic for MSMEs and Youth applicants.

3.3 Escalation Triggers

Arbiter must trigger human review if confidence in "But-For" logic is below 85%.

3.4 Interaction Standards

All communication via SMS/App must be respectful and provide a clear path to human support.

4.1 Measurable Accountability

Quarterly reports submitted to ODPC and CBK.

4.2 Continuous Improvement

Annual constitution review based on default rates and inclusion data.

Tier 5: Amendment & Governance

Amendments require 2/3 majority of the Board and ODPC approval.
