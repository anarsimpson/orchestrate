Tier 1: Statutory
1.1: Meaningful Explanation (Sec. 35)
The "But-For" Logic Mandate
Per Section 35 of the Data Protection Act, every automated denial must provide a certificate of explanation that allows the borrower to understand and rectify the underlying data.
fact_check Technical Rules for Arbiter
  • But-For Logic: Disclose the top 3 variables that, if changed, would have led to an approval.
  • Trace Audit Hash: Generate a unique identifier for every decision to verify it against this Constitution in an audit.
  • Human Recourse: Provide a 30-day window for manual review by a credit officer.
  • Data Lineage: List all third-party APIs (e.g., Safaricom, KRA) used in the millisecond of decisioning.
Tier 1
1.3: Latent Bias & Proxy Suppression
Moving beyond direct discrimination
The Arbiter must suppress variables that serve as proxies for protected groups, specifically targeting sub-location and hardware profiles.
  • Handset Suppression: Device type/model cannot be a primary reason for credit denial.
  • Geographic Fairness: Sub-location data must be aggregated to regional levels to prevent tribal/ethnic profiling.
  • The 5% Rule: Halt processing if regional approval disparities exceed 5% over a 24-hour window.
